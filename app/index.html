What Are Chargebacks?

Chargebacks are a forced transaction reversal initiated by the cardholder’s bank. Is simple terms, it’s the reversal of a credit card payment that comes directly from the bank. If you’re a consumer, chargebacks represent a shield between you and dishonest merchants.

The purpose of Chargebacks

On the outside, chargebacks can appear very similar to traditional refunds, yet there is one very relevant difference: rather than contact the business for a refund, the consumer is asking the bank to forcibly take money from the business’s account. An investigation follows, and if the bank feels the cardholder’s request is valid, funds are removed from the merchant’s account and returned to the consumer
